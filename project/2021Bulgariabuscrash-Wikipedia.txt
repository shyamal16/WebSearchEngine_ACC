(November 2021) (Learn how and when to remove this template message) 2021 Bulgaria bus crash Date November 23, 2021 Time 02:00 (UTC+2) Location Near Bosnek, Bulgaria Coordinates 42°29?27?N 23°07?53?E? / ?42.49083°N 23.13139°E? / 42.49083; 23.13139Coordinates: 42°29?27?N 23°07?53?E? / ?42.49083°N 23.13139°E? / 42.49083; 23.13139 Deaths At least 45 Non-fatal injuries 7 On November 23, 2021, at 02:00 local time, a North Macedonian bus crashed and caught fire in western Bulgaria.[1] The accident happened on the Struma Motorway near the village of Bosnek, south-west of Sofia.[2] 52 people were on the bus.[3] Forty-six people died during the crash, including twelve children. Seven other passengers suffered burns but survived.[4][5][6] It is currently the deadliest road accident in Bulgarian history.[7] The Dolna Dikanya to Dragichevo section of the Struma Motorway was temporarily closed following the incident.[4] Background[edit] According to European Commission data, in 2019 Bulgaria had the second highest road fatality rate in the EU. After the accident, the mayor of the nearby town of Pernik told local media outlets that the motorway was in poor condition and accidents were frequent in the area.[8][4] Accident[edit] A bus carrying Macedonian tourists returning to the capital Skopje from a weekend holiday trip to Istanbul, registered under the tourist agency "Besa Trans",[9] crashed on a motorway near the Bulgarian village of Bosnek.[10] The vehicle hit a highway barrier either before or after it caught fire.[4] Forty-six people died during the crash, including twelve children. The majority of the other victims were aged between 20 and 30. The Bulgarian Interior Minister Boyko Rashkov [bg] stated that the victims' bodies were "completely burned".[5] Seven passengers (five males and two females) managed to escape through windows with serious burns, and were hospitalized in critical condition.[4] Initial theories around the cause of the incident include driver error and technical malfunction.[4] The majority of the victims were Albanian citizens of North Macedonia.[11][12][13] References[edit] ^ Albeck-Ripka, Livia (November 23, 2021). The government declared Wednesday a National Day of Mourning for both victims of the crash and for the earlier deaths from a fire at a nursing home in Royak.
